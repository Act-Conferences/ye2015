Course 1. Web Application Development using Perl, HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript

  • Instructor: Gabor Szabo
    • Gabor has been using Perl since 1995 and teaching it since 2000. He has given Master classes at several previous Perl conferences. Both in North America and in Europe. Gabor runs the Perl Weekly newsletter, the Perl Maven web sites and the Code Maven web site. He is the author and maintainer of several Perl modules. He is one of the contributors of MetaCPAN.
    • http://szabgab.com/about.html
  • Abstract: You are going to learn how to create a mobile-enabled, responsive web application using the following pieces of technology: MongoDB, Perl Dancer 2 on the server HTML5/CSS3/JavaScript, Twitter Bootstrap on the front-end.
  • Class Description: This course is for you if from time to time you need to build internal web applications, or if you'd like to convert a CGI based application to modern technologies, or if you just want to be the next Twitter or Facebook.
    The course is 2 days long.
    Participants are expected to have basic understanding of HTML and at least intermediate knowledge of Perl.
    The students will get he training material in PDF format and they will get plenty of example files. They are expected to bring their own computer running Linux, Unix, OSX, or MS Windows. More specific instructions will be given a few days prior to the training class.
  • Duration: 2 days

    • Regular price: 400 Euro
    • The prices include the training material in printed and e-book format and lifetime access to the Perl Maven Pro subscription service. (Normally $90 / year).

Course 2. Practical Career Development for Perl Programmers: How to make more money and have more fun

  • Instructor: Pete Sergeant (see his Linkedin profile) from PerlCareers
  • Contents::
    • What makes a good CV; how to highlight your skills
    • Technical interview preparation; how to ask the right questions, give great answers to obscure HR questions about "where you see yourself", ask for the right amount of money, and answer questions like a politician being hunted down by Jeremy Paxman
    • Future-proofing your skill set; what you should be reading and hacking on when you're not at work, how to convince work to let you skill up on their time, common areas missing from CVs, and why BBC iPlayer includes IRC chat-bot code.
    • Street-fighting office politics; how to harness your natural cynicism and introversion to avoid getting fired and wage stagnation...
  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Date and hour: 1-September-2015, afternoon (exact hour to be determined)
  • Place: To be determined
  • Price: 150 Euro
